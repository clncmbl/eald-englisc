Notes

Consider the exclamative "How much we have heard..." for which the indicative would be "We have heard so much...".

Possible reading

How much we have heard about the glory of the nation-kings of the Spear-Danes of yore, about how the princes showed courage!

Scyld Scefing, with bands of warriors, often deprived many tribes of mead-benches and terrified the Heruli. After he first was found destitute, he received refuge, grew under heaven, and prospered with glory until every neighboring people beyond the sea had to listen to him and pay tribute. That was a good king!

Notes

The Heruli were a tribe that the Danes expelled from their Scandinavian homeland around the historical time period of this story. A case for emending "eorl" to "eorle" and translating as Heruli is made in "The Emendation Eorle (Heruli) in Beowulf, Line 6a: Setting the Poem in 'The Named Lands of the North'" by Drout and Goering.
