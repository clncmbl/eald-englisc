The story

Cynewulf and the West Saxon witan took from Sigebryht his rule, except over Hamptonshire, for unrighteous deeds. Sigebryht held Hamptonshire until he slew Cumbran, the aldorman who had remained with him the longest. Cynewulf then drove Sigebryht away to the Andred forest where he remained until a swineherd stabbed him to death at the stream at Privett, thereby avenging the aldorman Cumbran. As king, Cynewulf often fought in great battles against the Britons. Thirty one years into Cynewulf's rule, he decided to drive out an atheling named Cyneheard, who was Sigebryht's brother.
